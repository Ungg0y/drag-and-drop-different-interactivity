This course focuses on trap-setting questions, which are open-ended, two-sided questions that use McAfee defensible differentiators to satisfy the client’s required capabilities and to encourage the customer to perform due-diligence research to discover just what the competitor can or cannot do. 

One goal of a trap-setting question is to make the customer realize the competitor cannot do what McAfee can. While they correspond to discovery questions, trap-setting questions are unique, because they do what their name suggests, set traps for the competition. A good trap-setting question will draw out the customer value delivered by the defensible differentiators.

Remember, defensible differentiators answer a client’s why you or so what questions. They show how McAfee is either:

  • Unique, since we have it and no one else does
  • Comparative, in that we have the best version of it and while others may have it, we’re better in a meaningful way, or
  • Holistic, meaning we have a solution for the client’s entire table of concerns

These are the points that differentiate MVISION Cloud from the competition. Defensibility comes into play when a client asks, “Says who?” They want proof, and in this way, defensibility is like a proof point for the differentiators themselves.

This may seem overly complicated, since it is easy to directly attack the competition, but keep in mind a client may not appreciate an attack on a competitor they like. In fact, directly saying that a chosen vendor is a bad choice may make the client feel like their intelligence is being indirectly attacked. Instead, a trap-setting question encourages the client to think, research, and conclude a favorite competitor maybe isn’t as great as first thought.
